# Korean Onggi Pottery & Fermentation Vessel Traditions Registry

This repository preserves, in an immutable and decentralized format, the ceramic traditions of **onggi** — Korea’s porous, breathable pottery used for fermenting kimchi, doenjang, gochujang, soy sauce, and other foundational foods of Korean culture.

Onggi is not simply pottery: it is a **biological technology**, engineered over centuries to regulate oxygen exchange, moisture, and microbial activity. Its porosity, clay composition, and firing methods make it uniquely suited for fermentation.

## Why Onggi Matters

Onggi traditions represent:

- a **living fermentation technology**, optimized for lactic acid bacteria  
- a **regional craft system**, with Ulsan, Jeolla, and Gyeongsang as major centers  
- a **material science heritage**, using iron-rich clays and natural ash glazes  
- a **culinary foundation**, essential for kimchi, jang, and preserved foods  
- a **survivor of industrial decline**, maintained by master potters  

Onggi is recognized globally as one of the most advanced traditional fermentation vessels.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, censorship-resistant record** of onggi pottery traditions.  
2. **Preserve endangered ceramic knowledge** with technical accuracy.  
3. **Document materials, techniques, functional properties, and cultural context**.  
4. **Provide an immutable reference** for museums, researchers, artisans, and future generations.  
5. **Honor the regional diversity of Korean fermentation vessel craftsmanship.**

The contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no monetization, no barriers  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls  
- **permanent** — entries become part of the cultural ledger  

## What Each Entry Records

- **vesselName** — onggi, jangdok, hangari, kimchi-onggi  
- **region** — Ulsan, Jeolla, Gyeongsang  
- **materials** — porous clay, natural ash glazes  
- **techniques** — paddle-and-anvil, wheel throwing, low-temperature firing  
- **functionalProperties** — breathability, CO₂ release, microbial stability  
- **culturalContext** — kimchi, doenjang, gochujang, soy sauce  
- **uniqueness** — regional identity, biological optimization
